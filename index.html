About Me:

My name is Todd Schindler and I am a Computer Science graduate from the University of Washington. During my time at UW I focused heavily on AI, Vision, and Computer Graphics and I am looking forward to applying some of my creative and technical skills in the field this year! In 2024 and 2025 I worked at a non-profit community organization called the Duwamish Valley Sustainability Association (DVSA). At DVSA I was project manager of three different major projects. First was the installation and operation of the first community owned and operated biodigester , located at Foodlifeline. Concurrently, I directed about 16 students through an air quality monitoring course. After this I created a curriculum for video game development and 3D modeling. I taught this curriculum immediately after and in about three months the youth and I had created a basic 3D game. The game and the program are called Cuidadores de Agua, which roughly translates to caretakers of the water in English. We worked with the EPA to integrate research from their superfund project in the Lower Duwamish River into the content of our game.


Also, I was the primary grant writer, website manager, and final report writer at DVSA. I had to read/write both Spanish and English at the job and I studied Spanish for five years in school, so I have got pretty good at it.

Accomplishments:

Cuidadores de Agua Video Game

In 2024 and 2025 I created a curriculum and taught a class to eight students for four months about video game development basics. We went over coding, 3d modeling, and level design. At the end of the teaching portion of the program we tackled completing an educational game related to the EPA Superfund cleanup site in the Lower Duwamish River of Seattle. We cited EPA contacts and resources to create the educational aspects of the game. In the end we created an nine level long game based around the dredging of contaminated sediment in the river.
